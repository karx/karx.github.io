The Big Story

The Architecture of 'Viksit Bharat': Winter Session Transforms The Indian State

By The Political Bureau

Updated 2 hours ago

The Winter Session of Parliament has transcended routine legislative business to become a foundational moment in India's administrative history. Moving decisively away from the UPA-era "rights-based" framework, the government has unveiled a "mission-mode" architecture for a digitized, capital-intensive economy.

From repealing MGNREGA to ending the state monopoly on atomic energy, the session represents a concerted effort to dismantle long-standing statutory frameworks. The "Viksit Bharat" 2047 vision is now being codified into law.

What Is Happening

  • VB-G RAM G Bill Replaces MGNREGA. Increases work days to 125 but mandates a 60:40 (Centre:State) funding split.
  • SHANTI Bill Allows private sector in nuclear energy. Caps liability at 300M SDRs.
  • Education Overhaul Single regulator 'Adhishthan' to replace UGC & AICTE.

The "Ram" vs "Gandhi" Nomenclature War

The shift from MGNREGA to VB-G RAM G (Viksit Bharat Guarantee for Rozgar and Ajeevika Mission) has triggered a cultural firestorm.

Opposition claims it erases Mahatma Gandhi's legacy. The Government argues "RAM" is merely a functional acronym and accuses critics of being "allergic to Indian ethos."

Nuclear Liability: Privatizing Profit, Socializing Risk?

The SHANTI Bill aims for 100 GW nuclear capacity by 2047 by allowing 100% FDI and private operators.

Critics warn that capping liability at roughly ₹3,300 Crore for private operators leaves the state to cover catastrophic failures, effectively subsidizing corporate risk.

Deep Dive What Was Ignored

The Air We Breathe

Despite severe AQI levels in North India forcing school closures, the pollution crisis saw no substantive legislative response, reduced only to a partisan "blame game" between states.

Aviation Meltdown

The "humanitarian crisis" at airports due to IndiGo's operational collapse and pilot fatigue issues was mentioned but led to no regulatory intervention or structured debate.

The "Blind" Repeal

71 laws were repealed in minutes via voice vote. Opposition argues relatively recent laws were scrapped without adequate explanation of their "obsolescence."

Political Heat Index

SIR Controversy CRITICAL

"Detect, Delete, Deport" vs "Vote Chori". The Electoral Roll revision paralyzed Parliament.

Vande Mataram HIGH

150th Anniversary debate. BJP claims historical "truncation"; Opposition calls it a distraction.

Fiscal Federalism ELEVATED

States oppose the new 60:40 funding split in the rural employment bill, citing bankruptcy fears.

Did You Know?

  • • The Indian Tramways Act, 1886 was finally repealed this session among 71 other obsolete laws.
  • • Despite "Atmanirbhar Bharat", the trade deficit with China widened to $63.97 Billion (Apr-Oct).
  • • The new Insurance Bill raises FDI to 100% and allows "Composite Licenses" for life and non-life products.
